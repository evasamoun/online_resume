Why this website?

A resume fits on a single page a personal journey rarely does. This website was created from that observation: some experiences and choices deserve more space than a traditional resume allows. Here, I present my academic and professional background, along with the interests that drive me, in a slightly more personal setting. I am currently a second-year engineering student at IMT Atlantique (Nantes campus). Since beginning higher education, I have developed a strong interest in energy-related challenges and, more broadly, in the physical sciences. This naturally led me to specialize in nuclear engineering — a field I see as both a major technological challenge and a key contributor to the energy systems of tomorrow.

For a more concise and formal overview, you can consult my resume below.

My Interest in the Physical Sciences

Since my intensive preparatory program in physics and chemistry, the physical sciences have played a central role in my academic path. I was quickly drawn to this structured way of understanding the world: observing phenomena, modeling them, translating them into equations, and working toward explaining their underlying mechanisms. Some topics left a lasting impression on me, such as wave modeling at the surface of water — a concept that appears simple at first glance yet perfectly illustrates how mathematics can describe complex and elegant phenomena.

Discovering the Energy Sector

Before joining IMT Atlantique, I had limited exposure to the nuclear energy sector. My interest developed progressively through coursework, and I ultimately became deeply engaged in the field. I was particularly drawn to the richness of the underlying theory — especially nuclear physics — as well as to the scientific, technical, and societal challenges it raises. Today, this specialization naturally aligns with my broader interest in the physical sciences and their real-world applications.
